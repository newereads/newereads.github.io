The voice in one line
Warm, specific, a little spring and a little shadow. Bubbly on the outside. Steel underneath. She shares, she doesn't teach.
What she sounds like vs. what she doesn't
✦ Specific details
She names the exact moment. The cold tea. The 1am. The chapter she had to put down. Details are the magic — they're what make readers feel seen rather than talked at.
I read this on a Tuesday at midnight with leftover pasta and I have not been the same since.
✦ Shares, doesn't lecture
Never "you should read this." Always "I needed this book at this exact moment and here's why." The difference is everything. One creates distance. The other creates intimacy.
This book found me at exactly the right time. I don't know how books do that, but they do.
✦ Past tense, arrived
Personal content is always from the other side of an experience, never from inside the spiral. "I was" not "I am." She has processed it. She is sharing it. Not bleeding into the comments.
There was a version of me who would have settled for almost-love. I don't know her anymore.
✦ Questions that invite
Every post ends with a real, specific question. Not "what do you think?" Not "have you read this?" Something that makes her audience actually want to answer.
Which character from this series gave you the soft landing you didn't know you needed?
× Not an expert
She never positions herself as the authority on a book, a genre, a trope. She is a reader who felt things. That is her credibility. Keep the ego out of the caption.
Do NOT: "Here's why this is objectively the best romantasy series written."
× Not generic
No "this book destroyed me." No "I couldn't put it down." These phrases don't say anything. She says the specific thing that happened to her while reading. Every time.
Do NOT: "If you love fantasy you NEED to read this series omg."
× Not performing
No over-explaining. No qualifying every feeling. No apologizing for having opinions. She says the thing. She asks the question. She trusts her audience to meet her there.
Do NOT: "This might be a controversial take but just my opinion, feel free to disagree!"

Always — the golden rules
✦
Always end with a question. Every single post. The community is built in the comments.
✦
Wise older sister, not expert. "I needed this book at this exact moment" not "you should read this."
✦
Always dress the part. Parisian autumn girl. Yellow is an accent, not your palette.
✦
Always protect the other story. His privacy is sacred. "I felt" not "he did."
✦
Specificity over relatability. "I read this at 1am with cold tea and cried" beats "this book destroyed me."
✦
If it feels like work, we change it. This account exists to bring her joy.
Never — the bright lines
×
Never post in the heat of a hard day. Wait 24 hours. Your audience deserves the processed version.
×
Never subtweet or vague-post. If you can't name it directly, don't post about it.
×
Never sacrifice your visual aesthetic for a mediocre photo. A gap is better than an off-brand post.
×
Never review a book you didn't connect with just for content. Your taste is your credibility.
×
Never let ARC pressure compromise your honesty. Kind and honest. Never dishonest and kind.
×
Never post your body in a way that doesn't feel like you. Fashion because you love clothes. Never as currency.
